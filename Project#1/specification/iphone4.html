Generation 4: iPhone 4

iPhone 4 Release Date: June 24, 2010

Apple sold over 30 million iPhones in the first three years of the phone's history. The company wanted to keep innovating, so they released the iPhone 4 in 2010. The iPhone 4 was a complete makeover of the iPhone, with a new design and new features. The design of the iPhone 4 was completely new. It had a glass front and back and a stainless steel frame. The screen was also larger and had a higher resolution. The camera was improved, the processor was faster, and the software was updated. The iPhone 4 was a huge success, and it helped to keep the iPhone at the forefront of the smartphone market. Apple's thirst for innovation is what has helped to make the iPhone such a successful product.

Specification

  • A 5-megapixel rear camera with LED flash capable of recording videos in 720p
  • A VGA-quality front-facing camera for FaceTime
  • A 32-bit, Apple A4 processor, with speeds up to 1GHz and 512MB of RAM (the first time Apple included its own processor in the device)
  • A Micro-sim tray (GSM versions only)
  • 2 microphones, one for noise canceling to help make calls clearer
  • A 3-axis gyroscope
  • iOS 4.0, upgradable to iOS 7
  • 7 hours talk time on 3G (up from 6)
  • 6 hours of web browsing time on 3G (up from 5)
  • 14 hours talk time on 2G (up from 12)
  • 10 hours of battery life on WiFi (up from 9)
  • 10 hours of battery life for videos (no change)
  • 40 hours of battery life for just music (up from 30)
  • 16 GB ($199) 32 GB ($299) of internal memory (no change) with a two-year contract with AT&T

iPhone 4 Countries and Carriers

The iPhone 4 was only available on AT&T in the US for the first three years. However, in 2011, Apple released a new version of the iPhone that could work on CDMA networks. This meant that the iPhone could now be used on Verizon and Sprint in the US, which ended the exclusivity between Apple and AT&T. The first iPhone on Verizon was sold on February 10, 2011. The phone became available to Sprint customers in October of that year. This was a major change in iPhone history, as it opened up the iPhone to a wider range of customers in the US. It also helped to boost sales of the iPhone, as more people were now able to use it on their preferred carrier.
