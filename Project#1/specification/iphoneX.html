Generation 13: iPhone X

Release Date: November 3, 2017

Apple had been releasing iPhones that were very similar to the previous year's models for a few years. Then, they released the iPhone X, which was a major departure from the previous models. It had a new design, new features, and a new price tag. The iPhone X was more expensive than the previous models, but it was also more powerful and had better features. It was a commercial success, and it helped to revitalize the iPhone franchise. Apple also broke with recent tradition when releasing the iPhone X. They only offered one version of the phone, instead of the usual two or three versions. This was a risky move, but it paid off. The iPhone X was a hit with consumers, and it helped to show that Apple was still capable of innovating.

Specification

  • Two 12-megapixel rear cameras
  • A 7-megapixel TrueDepth front-facing camera which can recognize facial expressions and which powers Face ID
  • Augmented reality capabilities
  • An A11 Bionic processor features 6 cores, 2.4 GHz and 3GB RAM
  • iOS 11
  • Bluetooth 5.0
  • 21 hours of talk time
  • 12 hours of internet use
  • 13 hours of wireless video playback
  • 64 GB ($999) or 256 GB ($1149)
