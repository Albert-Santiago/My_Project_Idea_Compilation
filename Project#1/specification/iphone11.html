Generation 15.1: iPhone 11

Release Date: September 10, 2019

Apple's decision to return to the traditional numbering system for the iPhone 11 was a welcome change. The previous naming convention was confusing, and it was unclear whether Apple was transitioning to Roman numerals. The iPhone 11 is a minor upgrade over the iPhone XS, but it does have some new features. These include a new triple-lens rear camera system, a new Night mode for taking photos in low-light conditions, and a new U1 chip for improved spatial awareness. The iPhone 11 is also more affordable than the iPhone XS, starting at $699. This makes it a more attractive option for people who want the latest iPhone but don't want to spend a lot of money. Overall, the iPhone 11 is a good phone, but it's not a revolutionary upgrade. However, the new features are welcome, and the more affordable price tag makes it a more attractive option for many people.
